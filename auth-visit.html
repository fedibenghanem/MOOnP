About Us

Beekeeping and honey production are vital parts of the agriculture industry, but they are facing a significant threat from attacks by hornets, specifically hotnets. These attacks not only endanger the bees, but they also put the beekeepers at risk. Finding a solution to this problem is critical to protecting the bees and preserving this industry. Our mission is to find a way to interrupt hornet attacks without resorting to killing them or using toxic substances.

Danger honets

Vespa is a genus of social insects, in the family Vespidae, subfamily Vespinae, commonly known as hornets. The majority of species are native to Asia but some have spread to several continents, naturally or following introductions.
When hornet attacks result in the loss of bee colonies, beekeepers can experience significant economic losses. The loss of honey production and pollination services can also impact other agricultural industries.

Repellent Plants

There are several advantages to using repellent plants to defend against hornets. First and foremost, these plants offer a natural and eco-friendly solution to pest control, as they do not rely on harmful chemicals or pesticides that can be toxic to humans and animals. Repellent plants work by emitting natural scents or chemicals that deter hornets and other pests from approaching, making them a safe and effective alternative to chemical pest control methods.
